My Summer Internship

September 30,2021 by Alexandria Burnett

For the past two consecutive summers I have had the pleasure of interning at the Ministry of Transport and Mining. I thought I knew what to expect: the same filing that I've experienced at my past summer jobs. But, to my surpfise, even though I still had a lot of filing to do, I was also able to expand on my skills and actively experience what it was like to work in an environment similar to my chosen career.

During my second internship I was asked to come up with a program to assist in the managing of files in the office. Throughout the time I had been there, I had been assisting in file management for the department. On more than one occasion, I had been called to help searh for a file and had to flip through piles upon piles of paper to see if we had received it, stamped it and sent it out. The program I had been asked to create, would automate this process.

While I had written programs during my time at UWI, it was normally done in groupwork, but this time I was by myself. I found myself spending ages searching up how to do things: things I had forgotten like how to connect a button to things such as writing an NSIS script to install the program. At the end of my time there, I submitted the intial verison of my project. While grateful for the experience, I know there are a plethora of things that I could have spent more time on or made more efficient. Nevertheless, I am proud of what I created.

I am thankful for the opportunity to develop my skills and I hope that I can put the knowledge I have gathered from these internships to good use in my future work.

Close to the Finish Line

October 1,2021 by Alexandria Burnett

It's official! I'm now in my final year of university. If all goes well, I will be graduating with a degree in Computer Science in 2022. There were a lot of ups and downs on this path, but now the finish line is right in sight.

But even though I'm almost at the end of my university journey (for now at least), I have a lot of fear for the future.

Will I be successful?

Will I have a good job?

Will I like my job?

And there's no way for me to know for sure that the path that I am about to embark on is the right one. So although this finish line is a time for excitement and celebration, it is also a time for worry. In the years to come I can only hope that the skills and people I have met on my unviersity journey are sufficient in helping me move forward.
